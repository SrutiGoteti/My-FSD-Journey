# Web Design

## Principles of Web Design

1. Color Theory
- The science and art of creating the right palette for the website
  1. Mood
  - Red - Love, Energy, Intensity
  - Yellow - Joy, Intellect, Attention
  - Green - Freshness, Safety, Growth
  - Blue - Stability, Trust, Serenity
  - Purple - Rayalty, Wealth, Feminity

  2. Combining Colors
  - Analogous Colors - Choosing the colors that are right next to each other on the color palette. Ex: Nav Bar, Logo and it background
  - Complementary Colors - Creates pop, opposite colors in the color palette. Never style text and its background with this style.
  - Triadic colors - equilateral triangle color palette.
  - Square colors - square color palette.
  - [Link to Adobe Color Palette Generator](https://color.adobe.com/create/color-wheel)
  - Additionally, use Color Hunt
 
2. Typography
- Main two typographies:
  1. serif(have feet at the end of the central beams) - more serious, authoritative
  2. San-serif - friendly, approachable, sensible, straightforward, Humanist(for body ) is more legible
-  Combine only two fonts in the website
-  Combine fonts that have a similar mood, time-era, contransting(Serif and San-serif), weights bw heading and body

3. UI Design
- Hierarchy - highlight(make bold and big, and beautiful), the most imp info.
- Layout - 40-60 chars per line for text layout
- Alignment - reduce the number of alignments
- White Space - Empty space around elements(don't cramp)
- Audience

4. UX Design
- Effotless, simple design
- Consistency
- F-Layout
- Z-Laout
- All-platform-design
- Feedback
